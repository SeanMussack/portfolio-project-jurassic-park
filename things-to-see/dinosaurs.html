Dinosaurs

Come see the magestic creatures that have made Jurassic Park famous! The genetic engineers in our Laboratory are de-extincting new species all the time.

Brachiosaurus

Brachiosaurus is one of the tallest sauropods, or long-necked dinosaurs. Its front legs were longer than its rear legs, giving the dinosaur its name and allowing it to reach the highest treetops.

Diet:

Herbivore

Name Meaning:

"Arm Lizard"

Region:

North America

Epoch:

Late Jurassic

Dilophosaurus

What the small Dilophosaurus lacks in size, it makes up for in its spectacular coloration and deadly hunting capabilities, capable of spitting venom directly into its victim's eyes.

Diet:

Carnivore

Name Meaning:

"Two-Crested Lizard"

Region:

North America

Epoch:

Early Jurassic

Gallimimus

With long legs and a light build, the ostrich-like Gallimimus can reach running speeds of 50 km/h (30 mph). Unlike most therapods (two-legged dinosaurs), Gallimimus was a plant-eater.

Diet:

Herbivore

Name Meaning:

"Chicken Mimic"

Region:

East Asia

Epoch:

Late Cretaceous

Parasaurolophus

Like most hadrosaurs, or duck-billed dinosaurs, Parasaurolophus lived and moved in herds. It is known for its unique curved crest at the top of its head.

Diet:

Herbivore

Name Meaning:

"Near Crested Lizard"

Region:

North America

Epoch:

Late Cretaceous

Triceratops

With its bony frill and horns, Triceratops was well-equipped to defend itself from Tyrannosaurus attacks, though its horns are also thought to have been used in courtship display.

Diet:

Herbivore

Name Meaning:

"Three-Horned Face"

Region:

North America

Epoch:

Late Cretaceous

Tyrannosaurus

Although Tyrannosaurus was one of the largest carnivores to ever walk on dry land, visitors to Jurassic Park can rest assured that there is absolutely no threat of escape.

Diet:

Carnivore

Name Meaning:

"Tyrant Lizard"

Region:

North America

Epoch:

Late Cretaceous

Velociraptor

Watch out! Velociraptor is lightning fast, incredibly strong, and capable of executing coordinated hunting tactics. It was also definitely six feet tall and covered in scales, not feathers.

Diet:

Carnivore

Name Meaning:

"Swift Seizer"

Region:

East Asia

Epoch:

Late Cretaceous
